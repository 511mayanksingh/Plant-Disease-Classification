Plant Disease Information & Treatment

Comprehensive guide to identifying and treating crop diseases

🌶️

Pepper Bell - Bacterial Spot

Disease

Bacterial infection causing dark spots on leaves and fruit, reducing yield and quality.

  • Remove and destroy infected plants to prevent disease spread
  • Use copper-based fungicides to manage the disease
  • Practice crop rotation to reduce disease pressure
🌶️

Pepper Bell - Healthy

Healthy
  • Maintain proper cultural practices including adequate watering and fertilization
  • Continue regular pest management to prevent diseases
  • Monitor plants regularly for early disease detection
🥔

Potato - Early Blight

Disease

Fungal disease causing dark spots with concentric rings on leaves.

  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts showing symptoms
  • Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil or mancozeb
  • Practice crop rotation avoiding potatoes for several years
🥔

Potato - Late Blight

Disease

Serious fungal disease that can destroy entire crops rapidly.

  • Apply fungicides with mancozeb or copper-based products
  • Manage irrigation to minimize leaf wetness periods
  • Remove and destroy infected material promptly
🥔

Potato - Healthy

Healthy
  • Implement proper soil drainage and plant spacing
  • Maintain cultural practices for healthy plants
  • Regular monitoring for pest and disease prevention
🍅

Tomato - Bacterial Spot

Disease

Bacterial infection causing dark lesions on leaves and fruit.

  • Remove and destroy infected plant material
  • Apply copper-based fungicides preventatively
  • Practice crop rotation in affected areas
🍅

Tomato - Early Blight

Disease

Fungal disease affecting lower leaves first with characteristic rings.

  • Remove infected leaves especially near plant base
  • Apply chlorothalonil or mancozeb fungicides
  • Mulch around plants to reduce soil splashing
🍅

Tomato - Late Blight

Disease

Devastating fungal disease affecting leaves, stems, and fruit.

  • Apply chlorothalonil or mancozeb fungicides
  • Remove infected material promptly
  • Ensure good airflow to reduce humidity
🍅

Tomato - Leaf Mold

Disease

Fungal disease thriving in humid conditions with characteristic mold.

  • Improve ventilation by spacing and pruning plants
  • Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness
  • Apply copper or sulfur fungicides
🍅

Tomato - Septoria Leaf Spot

Disease

Fungal disease causing small spots with gray centers on leaves.

  • Remove and destroy infected leaves to reduce inoculum
  • Apply chlorothalonil or copper-based fungicides
  • Ensure proper spacing and ventilation
🍅

Tomato - Spider Mites

Pest

Tiny pests that suck plant juices, causing stippling and webbing.

  • Apply insecticidal soaps, neem oil, or horticultural oils
  • Use predatory mites for natural control
  • Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides harming beneficial insects
🍅

Tomato - Target Spot

Disease

Fungal disease creating distinctive target-like spots on leaves.

  • Remove and destroy infected leaves with target spot
  • Apply chlorothalonil or azoxystrobin fungicides
  • Improve air circulation through pruning and staking
🍅

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Viral

Viral disease transmitted by whiteflies causing leaf curling and yellowing.

  • Use resistant tomato varieties when available
  • Control whitefly populations with insecticides or reflective mulches
  • Remove and destroy infected plants
  • Implement good weed control practices
🍅

Tomato Mosaic Virus

Viral

Viral disease causing mottled, discolored leaves and reduced fruit quality.

  • Remove and destroy infected plants immediately
  • Control aphid populations transmitting the virus
  • Use resistant tomato varieties if available
  • Practice good sanitation to prevent mechanical transmission
🍅

Tomato - Healthy

Healthy
  • Maintain proper watering, fertilization, and pest management
  • Monitor plants regularly for signs of pests or diseases
  • Implement crop rotation to prevent pathogen buildup
